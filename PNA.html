OFFICIAL STUDY GUIDE

Agenda Item I

Legislating Oversight of Military Operations and Resource Allocation During the Pakistan–Afghanistan Conflict

Introduction

The Pakistan–Afghanistan conflict refers to the security, political, and military challenges along the Pakistan-Afghanistan border, particularly concerning terrorism, cross-border militancy, refugee movements, and border management. As Pakistan conducts military and counterterrorism operations to safeguard national security, questions arise regarding parliamentary oversight, accountability, transparency, and the effective allocation of public resources. The National Assembly plays a crucial role in ensuring that military expenditures and operations remain aligned with constitutional principles and national interests.

History & Background

  • Pakistan and Afghanistan have shared a complex relationship since Pakistan's independence in 1947.
  • The dispute over the Durand Line has remained a source of tension between the two countries.
  • Following the Soviet invasion of Afghanistan in 1979, millions of Afghan refugees entered Pakistan.
  • After the events of 9/11, Pakistan became a frontline state in the global War on Terror.
  • Militant groups such as the Tehrik-i-Taliban Pakistan (TTP) used Afghan territory to launch attacks within Pakistan.
  • The Taliban's return to power in Afghanistan in 2021 significantly altered regional security dynamics.
  • Pakistan has increased border fencing, military deployments, and counterterrorism operations to address security threats.
  • Increased military operations have resulted in growing defense expenditures, creating a need for stronger parliamentary oversight.

Main Problems

1. Limited Parliamentary Oversight

Military operations are often conducted with minimal direct scrutiny by elected representatives, raising concerns regarding transparency and accountability.

2. Resource Allocation Challenges

Large defense expenditures may reduce funds available for education, healthcare, infrastructure, and social welfare programs.

3. Border Security Threats

Cross-border terrorism, militant infiltration, and smuggling continue to threaten national security.

4. Civilian Impact

Military operations may affect civilians living in border regions through displacement, economic disruption, and security concerns.

5. Intelligence and Secrecy Concerns

While oversight is necessary, excessive disclosure of military information could compromise operational effectiveness and national security.

6. Refugee Management

Pakistan hosts millions of Afghan refugees, creating economic, social, and security challenges.

Committee Stakeholders

Government of Pakistan

Responsible for national security policy, budgeting, and diplomatic engagement with Afghanistan.

Pakistan Armed Forces

Conduct military and counterterrorism operations and manage border security.

National Assembly

Provides legislative oversight, approves budgets, and ensures accountability.

Border Communities

Residents of KPK and Balochistan directly affected by security developments.

Afghan Administration

A key actor in cross-border cooperation and regional stability.

Afghan Refugees

Affected by border policies, repatriation efforts, and security measures.

International Organizations

Organizations such as the United Nations monitor refugee issues, humanitarian concerns, and regional stability.

Analysis of Impacts

Economic Impacts

  • Increased defense spending places pressure on the national budget.
  • Border closures reduce bilateral trade and economic activity.

Social Impacts

  • Population displacement and humanitarian concerns in conflict areas.
  • Increased insecurity affects education and healthcare access.

Political Impacts

  • Debates regarding civil-military relations and democratic accountability.
  • Greater demand for transparency in national security decisions.

Security Impacts

  • Successful operations can reduce terrorist threats.
  • Poorly coordinated policies may increase regional tensions.

Feasible Solutions (Framework for Resolutions)

01. Parliamentary Oversight

Establish regular classified briefings to parliamentary defense committees regarding military operations and expenditures.

02. Budget Transparency

Require annual public reports summarizing defense expenditures while protecting sensitive information.

03. Outcome-Based Funding

Allocate defense funds based on measurable security outcomes and operational effectiveness.

04. Civilian Protection

Create compensation and rehabilitation programs for civilians affected by military operations.

05. Smart Border Systems

Strengthen border fencing, surveillance systems, and coordinated security measures.

06. Diplomatic Engagement

Maintain active dialogue with Afghanistan to address cross-border terrorism and security concerns.

Agenda Item II

Evaluating Government Measures to Stabilize Petroleum Prices Amid Global Oil Market Volatility

Introduction

Petroleum products are essential for transportation, industry, agriculture, and electricity generation. Global oil prices are highly volatile and are influenced by geopolitical conflicts, production decisions by oil-producing countries, supply chain disruptions, and economic conditions. Since Pakistan imports a significant portion of its petroleum needs, fluctuations in international oil prices directly affect domestic inflation, economic growth, and living standards.

History & Background

  • Pakistan has historically relied on imported crude oil and petroleum products.
  • Global oil crises in the 1970s demonstrated the vulnerability of oil-importing countries.
  • Rising international oil prices have repeatedly contributed to inflation and economic instability in Pakistan.
  • The government periodically adjusts fuel prices, taxes, petroleum levies, and subsidies to manage economic impacts.
  • Recent geopolitical conflicts, including the Russia-Ukraine war and instability in the Middle East, have increased global oil market uncertainty.
  • Fuel prices significantly influence transportation costs, electricity generation, and food prices throughout Pakistan.

Main Problems

1. Dependence on Imported Oil

Pakistan's heavy reliance on imports exposes the economy to international price fluctuations.

2. Macroeconomic Inflation

Higher fuel prices increase transportation and production costs, leading to broader inflation.

3. Fiscal Deficit & Strain

Fuel subsidies can heavily strain government finances and critically increase budget deficits.

4. Currency Depreciation

A weaker Pakistani Rupee compounding over time exponentially increases the baseline cost of importing petroleum products.

5. Energy Security Concerns

Supply chain adjustments and disruptions directly threaten long-term domestic stability.

6. Public & Social Dissatisfaction

Sharp fuel price increases immediately reduce household purchasing power and risk sparking social unrest.

Market & Legislative Stakeholders

Federal Government

Responsible for pricing policies, taxation frameworks, subsidies, and overall macro-economic management.

Ministry of Energy

The Petroleum Division explicitly regulates local policies and provides strategic oversight of the sector.

Oil Marketing Companies

Commercial OMCs that handle structural importing, country-wide distribution, and commercial retail sales.

Consumers & Citizens

General households who fundamentally absorb the immediate daily hit of fuel modifications.

Transport Sector

Public/private logistics operators completely locked into cascading cost spikes.

Industrial Sector

Local manufacturing units whose structural profit margins dictate market viability.

Oil Producers (OPEC+)

External multi-national syndicates and global sovereign states dictating standard supply capacities.

Evaluation of Impacts

Economic Impacts

  • Sharp adjustments to structural localized inflation metrics and everyday living items.
  • Increased manufacturing input costs slowing down target GDP expansion rates.

Social Impacts

  • Diminishing baseline purchasing parity options for the common public.
  • Disproportionately harsh spending curves enforced onto underprivileged demographics.

Political Impacts

  • Persistent administrative duress applied straight onto legislative authorities.
  • Aggressive demand for transparency regarding state fiscal policy management.

Energy Impacts

  • Compromised safety margins when dealing with surprise global energy blockades.
  • Strategic emphasis forced toward planning structural renewable transitions.

Feasible Solutions (Framework for Resolutions)

01. Targeted Assistance Subsidies

Redirect fiscal packages to isolated underprivileged communities via automated support channels instead of open broad discounts.

02. Strategic Petroleum Reserves

Build physical emergency reserves to artificially support stability margins when international suppliers choke production.

03. Green Energy Diversification

Systematically scale wind, hydro, solar, and civil nuclear grids to loosen foreign market dependency parameters entirely.

04. Pricing Transparency Metrics

Enforce mathematical data visibility explaining tax percentages and refinery margins transparently to the general public.

05. Public Transport Logistics

Fund inner-city commuter lines to aggressively shift citizens off private vehicle consumption dependency models.

06. Macro Fiscal Protections

Incorporate state trading architectures to strengthen structural exchange balance models against foreign deficits.

Key National Assembly Perspective Directive

For both agendas, delegates must strictly center arguments around the National Assembly's constitutional mandate regarding legislation, budgeting, legislative oversight, institutional accountability, and macro policy formulation, rather than executing operational military commands or standalone executive adjustments. High-tier resolutions should dynamically balance national security parameters against democratic oversights in Topic A, and consumer relief programs against state fiscal responsibilities in Topic B.
